Eggs Benedict, The Queen of Breakfast

Description

Dubbed The Queen of Breakfast, this dish is a beautiful gem amongst the rubble of other egg dishes.

Cooked to perfection by the tried-and-true classic recipe, Erina added a secret weapon to it: Karasumi powder to further enhance the egg theme. One bite will instinctively cause servility to Erina.

Ingredients

  • Ground Chive
  • Hollandaise Sauce
    • Egg Yolks
    • Lemon Juice
    • Melted Butter
    • Water
  • Poached Egg
  • Oven Baked Bacon
  • English Muffin
    • Karasumi Powder
      • Dried Mullet Roe
      • Dried Various Fish Eggs
        • Salt

Steps

  1. Pour water and vinegar into a pot. Bring to a boil and then reduce heat.
    Stir the liquid with a spoon to create a small swirl, and then crack one ehh into that swirl.
    After 2 minutes, scoop the egg out with a slotted spoon and douse in cool water. Repeat step for all eggs.
  2. Make the Hollandaise sauce. put the egg yolks in a double boiler, and while they are warming, mix with a hand mixer.
    Remove once room temperature.
  3. Melt the butter in the microwave and split into 4 equal portions. Slowly add to the Hollandaise sauce and mix thoroughly.
    Add the worcestershire sauce, salt, pepper and lemon juice, and mix thoroughly to finish the sauce.
  4. Cut the bacon slices into quarters and cook in a frying pan until browned in both sides.
  5. Slice the english muffins in half. Toast and spread with butter. Top with the sauce.
  6. Dry the poached egg from step 1 and place on the top of the english muffin. Drizzle the Hollandaise sauce on top and sprinkle with chopped parsley and grated karasumi.
  7. Place on a plate, decorate with a lettuce leaf, and serve.
